DAESANG GROUP

1956

Founded in 1956, Daesang Corporation has become one of the largest fermentation businesses in the world and the leading consumer food producer in Korea with annual sales revenue of 2 billion dollar’s. Daesang employs nearly 3,000 people and maintains 25 subsidiaries around the globe.

As the center of the fermentation industry of Korea, Gunsan plant is developing into the production base for various fermentation products based on its state of the art facilities and the best technologies, and it is concentrating its efforts in expanding its overseas market share by equipping itself with international competitiveness in terms of quality and cost such as collecting, refining and processing based on its core fermentation technology

CHUNG JUNG ONE

1996

Chung Jung One was founded in 1996 by Daesang Group. 'Chung' defines freshness of nature, 'Jung' meaning rich in quality, and 'One' translates to wholesome. The objective of this company is to provide healthy ingredients for families to nourish their bodies. ChungjungOne studies the healthiness, tastiness, and freshness of food products, constantly researching on how to provide beyond better. The leaf brand symbol represents a meaning of 'healthy taste' - which leads back to the company's vision statement.

GOCHUJANG SAUCE

late 1800s

"Gochujang has been a Korean household staple for centuries, with its first use in Korea traced to the late 18th century. Traditionally fermented over years in large earthen pots outdoors, Chung Jung One has already been producing Gochujang for several decades. Chung Jung One’s new take on Gochujang launches the Korean chili sauce for the mainstream consumer, however—preserving the umami flavor that comes from the product’s fermentation with a natural balance of sea salt and sweetness provided by cane sugar."

"The vegan, gluten-free sauce now comes ready to use in a distinctive red 7.5-oz. bottle, and its many uses include marinating meats, adding flavor to stews and soups, and it can also be used as a hot sauce condiment. It brings a full range of spicy, savory, and sweet flavors to any dish—from traditional bibimbap to chicken wings, barbecue sauce, dips, and more."
